Overview

Urban Heat Island (UHI) is one of the clearest examples of human-induced climate modifications. The advent of thermal remote sensing has provided large-scale observations of urban environment, which greatly benefit the surface urban heat island (SUHI) studies. However, the nature deficiency of satellite data leave challenges in the SUHI investigation based on satellite observations, and my recent researches mainly attempted to address these challenges.

Current Projects

Attribution analysis of the daily variations in the nighttime surface urban heat island

        -Vast variaions on the SUHI was demonstrated, not only over the inter-annual/intra-annual scale, but also on the day-to-day scale
        -The day-to-day variations in the SUHI was mainly controlled by meteorological variables.
        -A paper related to this project was finished (Jiameng Lai is the first author), and was under the second view by Remote Sensing of Environment.

Simulating the next-day nighttime surface urban heat island

        -A statistical approach was proposed to simulate the next-day nighttime surface urban heat island.
        -Simulators related to the SUHI day-to-day variations largely contribute to the simulation.
        -A paper related to this project was finished (Jiameng Lai is the first author), and was under view by ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing.

Past Projects

        of 18 years data in the worldwide, and then I wrote a report, supervised by Professor Wenfeng Zhan of

        -The project was completed by myself

03/2017 – 12/2018        Innovation Training Program: Research on Qinhuai River Basin Health | Nanjing University, China

        -Supervised by Professor Youpeng Xu

        -Water quality data, land use data and socio-economic data were used to calculate the Water Surface Rate,
        Water Quality Comprehensive Index and Green Space Coverage Rate, Shannon’s Diversity Index, Growth Rate
        of Agricultural and Forestry Output Value and Water Consumption per Ten Thousand yuan of GDP, respectively,
        so as to make a comprehensive evaluation of river health in Qinhuai River Basin.

        -I am responsible for dividing the study area and calculating the Green Space Coverage Rate and Shannon’s Diversity
        Index from the land use data. Firstly, the study area was divided into 10 small watersheds through administrative
        division boundaries in ArcGIS. Secondly, land use data were overlayed with the divided 10 small watersheds in ArcGIS
        to calculate the Green Space Coverage Rate. Finally, fragstats was used to calculate the Shannon’s Diversity Index
        of each watershed
